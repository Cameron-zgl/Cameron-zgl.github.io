Ultrasound–Diabetes Multimodal Prediction

University of Michigan, Graduate Researcher (PI: Prof. Liyue Shen), 2025.03 – Present.

Built a unified pipeline linking clinical variables (e.g., BMI, age, labs) with skeletal-muscle ultrasound (deltoid, vastus; SAX/LAX views) for diabetes and insulin-resistance prediction. Trained clinical-only baselines and developed segmentation models (U-Net/MedSAM) to derive quantitative muscle features (echogenicity/density, thickness, cross-sectional area), with radiomics/texture descriptors and patient-level cross-validated evaluation. Designed and compared early- and late-fusion multimodal strategies to predict insulin resistance / type 2 diabetes.

Biomedical Pathology RAG Benchmark (DIAG)

University of Michigan, Multidisciplinary Design Program (MDP), Team Lead, 2025.01 – Present.

Lead a subteam designing a pathology-specific retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) benchmark for biomedical and pathology LLMs, integrating BioLLM and DeepSeek models with modular retrievers and embeddings. Developed standardized datasets and retrieval/generation evaluation metrics (e.g., Recall@K, nDCG, faithfulness) together with evidence-citation pipelines for benchmarking.
