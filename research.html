Research Interests

My research centers on making multimodal AI systems trustworthy enough for real clinical deployment — spanning medical vision-language modeling, model editing, and biomedical data science.

Medical AI & Biomedical Foundation Models

Developing and evaluating large-scale AI models for clinical imaging, biomedical text, and structured health data, with an emphasis on methods that generalize across institutions, modalities, and patient populations.

Medical Vision-Language Models

Studying image-grounded clinical reasoning in VLMs — how these models connect visual evidence in radiographs, CT, ultrasound, and pathology images to free-form clinical questions and answers.

Model Editing

Designing lightweight, targeted methods to correct post-deployment factual errors in large multimodal models without costly full retraining — and building evaluation frameworks that capture the locality, generality, and reliability trade-offs of different editors.

Trustworthy & Reliable AI

Investigating robustness under distribution shift, compositional reasoning, and temporal consistency, with the goal of building evaluation protocols suited to safety-critical settings rather than static benchmark accuracy alone.

Medical Visual Question Answering

Constructing and stress-testing Med-VQA systems (VQA-RAD, SLAKE, PathVQA, PMC-VQA) across image variation, question rewrites, and modality shift to better reflect real-world clinical variability.

Benchmark & Evaluation Design

Building reproducible evaluation stacks — pre/post-edit scoring, automatic grading, evidence attribution, and audit sampling — that support ablation-driven analysis instead of anecdotal case studies.

Multimodal Biomedical Data Science

Fusing imaging, clinical variables, and structured/text data (e.g., ultrasound radiomics with clinical labs, pathology reports with retrieval-augmented generation) for prediction and discovery tasks.

Research Vision

A guiding theme across my projects is human-centered, multimodal AI for healthcare: combining heterogeneous data sources (vision, language, structured records) with techniques for alignment, editing, and continual learning, evaluated not just on accuracy but on faithfulness, robustness under distribution shift, and reproducibility.

My past and current projects — medical multimodal model editing and evaluation, pathology RAG with provenance, and ultrasound × clinical data fusion — feed into a common direction: multimodal learning and foundation models that are robust, editable, and trustworthy enough to broaden access to health intelligence.
